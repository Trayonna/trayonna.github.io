Amazon Fulfillment Centers Are Adding Up

By Trayonna Hendricks

Amazon just keeps getting bigger. With over 140 fulfillment centers in the United States alone, in 2019 more are scheduled to join the party. New York is on that party list, with 855,000 square feet of real estate becoming apart of Amazons collection of massive fulfillment centers. Amazon already has fulfillment centers in 17 states, New York will make that list 18.

“Our demand is growing pretty rapidly and we are always focusing on trying to get as close to the customer,” Assistant General Manager Chris Colvin told CBS2’s Clark Fouraker. Amazon is only the second U.S company to employ more than half a million people, Walmart currently employs 2.2 million people around the world.

In 2018 the company faced criticism for its pay disparity, according to CNBC, Amazon’s starting pay varies by location, for example, $10 an hour at a warehouse in Austin, Texas, and $13.50 an hour in Robbinsville, New Jersey. Amazon says they plan to hire 2,500 associates for the Staten Island location, their website has yet to post any job openings for the facility.
